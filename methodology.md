# Methodology

## Research Objective

The objective of this research was to identify Bengaluru-based companies that align with the assignment's concept of a “Federer Company”.

For this assignment, I interpreted a Federer Company as an organisation that demonstrates:

* strong product ownership,
* engineering depth,
* systems thinking,
* operational complexity,
* innovation-driven execution,
* and long-term growth potential.

The focus was on identifying product-first and deeptech-oriented companies rather than generic outsourcing or service-based businesses.

---

## Research Process

The research process was completed in four stages:

### Stage 1 – Understanding the Assignment

I first reviewed the assignment guidelines carefully to understand the expected characteristics of a Federer Company.

Based on my understanding, I focused on companies that:

* build products instead of only offering services,
* solve technically complex problems,
* operate in engineering-intensive industries,
* and depend heavily on systems and process optimisation.

---

### Stage 2 – AI-Assisted Discovery

AI tools were used only as research accelerators and idea-generation assistants.

AI helped me:

* discover candidate companies,
* identify industry clusters,
* organise research categories,
* and speed up the initial exploration process.

The primary categories explored were:

* Robotics
* Aerospace
* Electric Vehicles
* Industrial AI
* MedTech
* Advanced Manufacturing

---

### Stage 3 – Manual Verification

All shortlisted companies were manually verified before inclusion.

Verification sources included:

* official company websites,
* LinkedIn company profiles,
* startup databases,
* public business directories,
* and media coverage.

The following aspects were verified whenever possible:

* Bengaluru presence or headquarters,
* product ownership,
* engineering focus,
* operational complexity,
* and relevance to the assignment criteria.

---

### Stage 4 – Filtering and Final Shortlisting

After verification, companies that did not strongly align with the assignment criteria were removed.

Examples of rejected categories:

* generic IT outsourcing firms,
* marketing agencies,
* coaching platforms,
* low-engineering service businesses,
* and companies with weak product ownership.

The final shortlist prioritised companies that demonstrated:

* innovation,
* technical depth,
* systems-level execution,
* and scalable product development.

---

## AI Usage

AI was used only as a research support tool.

Its role was limited to:

* discovering potential companies,
* generating initial research directions,
* identifying industry clusters,
* and helping structure information.

AI was not treated as the final source of truth.

All final decisions were based on manual review and verification.

---

## Hallucination Control

Since AI systems can generate inaccurate or incomplete information, several safeguards were applied during the research process.

To reduce hallucination risk:

* company headquarters were manually verified,
* unsupported claims were removed,
* multiple sources were cross-checked,
* company websites were reviewed directly,
* and generic outsourcing firms were filtered out.

I also avoided using information that could not be independently verified through public sources.

---

## Where I Disagreed With AI

During the research process, I disagreed with several AI-generated suggestions.

Some common issues observed were:

* AI suggested generic IT service companies that did not match the assignment criteria.
* Some companies had Bengaluru operations but were headquartered elsewhere.
* Certain descriptions exaggerated innovation claims or company scale.
* Some startups were presented as product companies despite having limited evidence of proprietary products.
* A few suggestions lacked sufficient public information for reliable verification.

As a result, multiple AI-generated recommendations were rejected before creating the final shortlist.

Final inclusion decisions were based on manual verification rather than AI confidence.

---

## Key Observations

During the research process, I observed several recurring patterns among Bengaluru’s strongest deeptech companies.

Key observations included:

* Hardware and software integration is common across robotics, aerospace, EV, and industrial automation companies.
* Founder-led engineering cultures are highly visible in many successful deeptech startups.
* Product-focused companies communicate differently from outsourcing firms, often emphasising technology, manufacturing, deployment, and operational execution.
* Bengaluru has a strong concentration of deeptech talent supported by research institutions, startup ecosystems, and industrial networks.
* Systems thinking and process optimisation appear to be important competitive advantages across multiple industries.

These observations influenced the final company selection and helped align the shortlist with the assignment objectives.
